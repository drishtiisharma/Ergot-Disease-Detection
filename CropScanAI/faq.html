FAQ(Frequently Asked Questions)

Quick answers to your biggest questions about detecting and preventing ergot in pearl millet.

What is ergot and why is it harmful?

Ergot is a fungal disease that affects cereal crops like pearl millet. It produces toxic alkaloids that can cause serious health problems if consumed, including convulsions, hallucinations, and even death in severe cases.

How does this tool detect ergot?

Our tool uses advanced AI image recognition trained on thousands of images of healthy and ergot-infected millet. It analyzes the visual characteristics of your uploaded images to identify potential ergot contamination.

What should I do if ergot is detected?

Separate and safely dispose of infected grains. Do not mix them with healthy ones.

Can ergot-infected grains be consumed or sold?

No. They are toxic and unsafe for food, feed, or sale.

How can I prevent ergot infection in the future?

Use clean seeds, rotate crops, and harvest on time. Avoid planting in infected soil.

What kind of images should I upload for detection?

Upload clear, close-up photos of the millet grains in good lighting.

Is this tool free and who can use it?

Yes. Anyone can use it for free to check their millet for ergot.
